History

Panangad Vocational Higher Secondary School, which took its inspiration from noble ideas of Shree Gopalamenon and Shree Kuttan Nair came into being in 1919 at Panangad. The school was established near Kammothu Bagavathy Temple, which started with a lower primary section consisting of a student population of 100 and 3 teachers which was named as Vinjanodayam Lower Primary School. It then, started growing from strength to strength when it reached its next stage of development.

In 1925, Vinjanodayam Middle School came in to being and training for music, stitching, drawing, and Arabic classes were started. In 1940, the management was taken over by Shree Govindankutty Menon. By 1960, the school manager Shri Velakadavil Gopinath Menon took charge as the school headmaster, which then led to a great progress in the development of the school infrastructure. He was a remarkable figure in the history of Panangad, who brought incredible developments in the field of education. Later in 1997, Panangad Vocational Higher Secondary School was established , which was inaugurated by Education Minister Shree Nalakathu Soopi. Since then, the school started gaining its strength and is now one of the most popular school in this area, with a strength of 2250 students and a teacher population of 120 and above, with an aim to impart the best of education, providing a platform for students to earn knowledge and develop the bidding talents within them.
